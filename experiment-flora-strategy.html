AI — Strategy

AI Brand Strategy

2025 Flora AI Personal Experiment

Strategy is the part of brand work that's hardest to shortcut — until now. This experiment tested how far AI could go in building a brand strategy from first principles.

The Brief

Subject: Bliss Club — an Indian activewear brand with strong product love and almost no distinct cultural territory. The objective was to evolve it from a product-led brand into a meaning-led lifestyle brand. But instead of handing this to a generic AI assistant and asking for a strategy deck, I wanted to see what happens when you build the right team first.

Building the Agents

Flora AI lets you define custom system prompts — agent personas — before running any tasks. So before a single strategy question was asked, I built five: Brand Strategist, Art Director, Brand Copywriter, Brand Designer, and Brand Researcher.

Each one had a specific worldview embedded into the prompt. Not just a job title, but beliefs, operating principles, and creative convictions. The difference between "you are a strategist" and "you are a strategist who treats brands as living systems, not visual veneers" is the difference between competent output and opinionated thinking. When an agent has a point of view, its output carries one.

The Art Director was prompted to make choices, not moodboards — to believe that a brand is a world, not wallpaper. The Researcher was built to be detail-dense, to separate signal from noise. Each agent was essentially a creative collaborator with a brief of their own.

The Workflow

With agents configured, I ran a structured chain of prompts through the canvas — each one building on the last, each output feeding the next node downstream.

Brief elaboration → competitive audit → India market analysis → consumer profiling → whitespace mapping → watch-outs → first brand thesis → critique loop → refinement → tone of voice → art direction thesis → visual identity → activation copy for OTT, OOH, quick commerce, POS.

The key wasn't just having an AI answer each question. It was building a system where answers compound. By the time the brand thesis was written, it had the weight of everything that came before it — the market context, the consumer insight, the competitive gap. It read like a real brief because it was built like one.

What Held

The quality of any output traces back to the quality of the agent prompt. Vague inputs produce work that's fine but forgettable. When you put real creative conviction into the system — specific beliefs, a clear operating logic, an actual POV — that conviction travels through the chain.

The other unlock was using the canvas as a thinking surface, not just a generation engine. Seeing how outputs connected across nodes changed how I thought about the strategy itself. The structure forced clarity in a way that open-ended prompting never does.

This wasn't AI doing the work. It was AI participating in it — at pace, at depth, without losing the thread.
