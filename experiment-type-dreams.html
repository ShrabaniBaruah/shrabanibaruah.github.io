I mapped twelve brand personalities that had appeared in real client briefs but resisted existing typeface solutions:

Rebellious luxury. Quiet authority. Joyful precision. Aggressive calm. Nostalgic futurism. Warm minimalism. Anxious confidence. Tender ambition. Scientific warmth. Subversive tradition. Elegant chaos. Earned lightness.

Each one describes a productive tension — two qualities that don't usually share a letter. That tension is where the most interesting letterforms live.

For each personality, I generated dozens of letterform explorations using Midjourney — focusing on single letters (usually 'a', 'g', 'R', and '&') because they carry the most personality in a typeface.

The most interesting outputs were never directly usable: inconsistent metrics, unresolvable internal spacing, structural impossibilities that would make a type designer wince. But they were extraordinary as starting points — directions toward letterforms, not letterforms themselves.

The ones that survived I traced and refined by hand in Illustrator. Three explorations eventually became the basis for custom lettering treatments in actual client work.

Very little, technically. AI image models have no understanding of typographic structure — of overshoot, contrast, optical adjustments, the logic that makes a font work at 8pt and 80pt simultaneously. What they have is an associative understanding of feeling: how certain visual qualities have been deployed historically, what emotional register a given set of forms tends to occupy.

That's actually useful. Art direction is also mostly associative. The technical rigour comes later, from craft.

Partner with a type designer from the start rather than trying to resolve the structural issues alone. The model suggests directions; a designer decides which ones are real. The conversation between those two perspectives is where the actual work happens.

I'd also spend more time on the personalities themselves — the twelve I started with were too broad. "Rebellious luxury" needs to be more specific: rebellious-against-what, luxury-in-whose-definition. The narrower the personality, the more precise the letterform.
