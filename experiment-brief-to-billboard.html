AI — Campaign

Brief to Billboard
in 48 Hours

2024 Claude, Midjourney, DALL·E, Runway, Photoshop Personal Experiment · Real Brief

A standard campaign takes 6–10 weeks. This experiment compressed the entire pipeline to 48 hours — not to replace the process, but to understand what it was actually protecting.

Hours 0–4: Insight synthesis. Fed the brief, category landscape, and competitive positioning into Claude. Generated audience personas, cultural tensions, and strategic territories in under two hours — work that typically takes a week of research and synthesis.

Hours 4–12: Territory development. Used the strategic output to generate 6 campaign territories with headlines, tone-of-voice notes, and one-line rationales. Iterated on the two strongest territories with rapid copy variants.

Hours 12–30: Visual executions. Generated key visuals using Midjourney and DALL·E across both territories. Refined in Photoshop. Produced OOH mockups, digital formats, and social cuts.

Hours 30–48: Review and rationalisation. Assembled into a presentation deck. Wrote the creative argument for each direction.

The 48-hour version had more concepts, more copy variants, more visual options than any standard 6-week process would have generated. The deck was comprehensive. The executions were technically competent.

And it was emotionally empty.

Not bad. Not wrong. Empty. The kind of work that has no argument, no point of view, no reason to exist beyond the brief. Work that could have been made for any brand in any category. Work that a client would approve because it ticks every box and leaves them with nothing to remember.

The 6–10 weeks isn't about production time. It's about conviction time. The time it takes for a creative team to become genuinely invested in a problem — to find the angle that isn't in the brief, to disagree productively with the strategy, to arrive at a point of view that is theirs and not just the brief's.

What the process protects isn't quality. It's ownership. Work with ownership has an argument behind it. Work without ownership is a technically correct response to a set of requirements.

AI produces the latter at extraordinary speed. The former still requires time and people.

What AI produces in 48 hours is the equivalent of a well-briefed junior team's first pass. It's not the work. It's the beginning of the work — the raw material that a creative director then needs to interrogate, reject, recombine, and argue with until something with a point of view emerges.

The experiment didn't show me that the process is inefficient. It showed me exactly why the process exists, in a way that six years of working through it never quite did.

I'd run this experiment again. It's the most useful thing I've done with AI.
